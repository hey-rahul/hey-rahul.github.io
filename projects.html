Experimental Study on the Effects of Input Parameters on Machinability of Maraging Steel 250 in Wire Electrical Discharge Machining Process

Guide: Mr. Manish Kumar (Scientist-F)

Feb 2020 - Mar 2020

We have investigated the effect of input parameters such as, pulse-on time, feed rate, servo control reference mean voltage, on machining performance of the wire electrical discharge machining (WEDM) process by observing the variation in output parameters namely, surface roughness, material removal rate, and average kerf width.

The material chosen for study is maraging steel 250, which is extensively used in military applications, for example for missile parts. This project is done at Defence Research & Development Laboratory, Hyderabad.

Taguchi method is used to analyse the experiment. Based on the experimental values, a regression model was created. Finally, response optimization was done to get optimum values for a given set of conditions.

Design and Analysis of Double Open-Ended Spanner using CATIA V5 R20: Part Design and Ansys R19.1

Guide: Mr. B. Naresh Kumar, Assistant Professor, CMR College of Engineering & Technology

May 2019 - Jul 2019

Spanner is designed using CATIA product version V5 R20 and ANSYS product version 19.1 Release.

A wrench or spanner is a tool used to provide grip and mechanical advantage in applying torque to turn objects—usually rotary fasteners, such as nuts and bolts—or keep them from turning. Higher quality wrenches are typically made from chromium-vanadium alloy tool steels and are often drop-forged. They are frequently chrome-plated to resist corrosion and for ease of cleaning.

In this project static structural analysis is conducted on a spanner. A three-dimensional model of the spanner is designed using CATIA V5 R20. The model is designed as a part design in CATIA and then a suitable material is added to it, here we have taken the material as steel.

Finite element analysis (FEA) is performed to obtain the variation of stress magnitude at critical locations of spanner. The static analysis is done using ANSYS R19.1. A load of 1 kN is applied to the spanner model in ANSYS R19.1, and boundary conditions are applied according to the most usual way the tool is used. The analysis is done to find the total deformation, equivalent stress and von Mises stress max yields criteria stress acting on the spanner.

Calibration of KUKA (KR 16) Industrial Robot for Drawing

Guides: Mr. Ravi Kishore & Mr. G. Karthik, Professors, Chennai Institute of Technology, Chennai

May 2018

The art of drawing a simple sketch of a logo is taught to the robot KUKA (KR 16) through an efficient program and calibration. This project is also aimed to show the flexibility of the robot.

Behind the project

We, a total of 15 students from our college went to Chennai Institute of Technology, Chennai and had undergone training on Industrial Robots, Automation and 3D Printing Technology. Calibrated and programmed KUKA KR-16 industrial robot to sketch a character and executed successfully.

Then visited Gokul Autotech Pvt. Ltd. (Royal Enfield 350cc, 500cc crankshaft assembly manufacturing plant) as industrial visit.
